What is the difference between Single conjugation verbs and Multi conjugation verbs?

Single conjugation verbs have a single stem or one way to conjugate it. These verbs typically appear in the passe composee and the conditionnel.

Some examples of this are: être in the passe composee: [j'ai été, tu as été]. it only has the 1: helping verb (avoir) which does not change, and 2: the past participle (été) which also does not change.

Another example is faire in the conditional: je FERais, tu FERais, il FERait, nous FERions. the stem fer is all you have to remember, as the endings are constant.

Multi conjugation verbs have a different conjugation for each pronoun. These verbs are typically in the present tense.

For example, the verb aller in the present tense: je vais, tu vas, il va, nous allons, vous allez, ils allent. it has a different conjugation for each pronoun.
